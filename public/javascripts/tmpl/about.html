About ONE TROOP

Hundreds of thousands of veterans are returning home after service in Afghanistan and Iraq. Every one of them has trouble adjusting back to civilian life, because not only is the reality is vastly different from army service, each one of them suffering from physical and mental toll of war. Most of them leave for service in the late teens, come back in their early 20s, so they’re not fully socially or emotionally developed. Here comes a most haunting statistic - in the last few years, more of them died from suicide than from combat, mostly due to post traumatic stress disorder

After all the injuries, struggles, hardships, they’ve been through, they’re looking for one, and one most important thing, that everyone of take granted.

Connection. Emotionally and spiritually. You can have mental health and suicide prevention programs, and all the resources in the world, but nothing beats community.

So we thought about how we can enable this community specifically for returned army forces. This is what we came up with:

We made a simple platform for soldiers to tell stories. Stories are powerful, stories arouse, and most of all, stories connect.
